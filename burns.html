Burns

  • Basic information and Why it happened

    • Burns occur when the skin or other tissues are damaged due to exposure to heat, electricity, chemicals, or radiation. Burns can range from mild to severe, and the severity depends on the depth and extent of tissue damage. Common causes of burns include scalds from hot liquids, contact with flames or hot objects, and exposure to chemicals.

  • Prevention Advice

    • Preventing burns involves taking appropriate safety measures to avoid exposure to burn hazards. To prevent burns, it's important to use caution when handling hot liquids, flames, and hot objects. Keep flammable materials away from heat sources, and use appropriate protective equipment when working with chemicals or other hazardous materials.
    Install smoke detectors in your home and maintain fire extinguishers in easily accessible areas.

  • Damage to the Body

    • The damage caused by burns depends on the severity and extent of the burn. Mild burns may cause redness, pain, and swelling, while severe burns can cause tissue damage, scarring, and even death.
    Burns can lead to shock, a condition where the body's vital organs do not receive enough oxygen or nutrients, and can result in organ damage or failure.

  • What to Do

    • If you or someone else has been burned, it's important to take the following steps:

    1. Call for emergency medical help and Seek medical attention immediately.
    2. Remove the person from the source of the burn.
    3. If clothing is on fire, use a blanket, towel, or other material to smother the flames.
    4. Cool the burned area with cool running water for at least 20 minutes, until the pain subsides.
    5. Remove any clothing or jewelry from the burned area, but do not remove anything that is stuck to the skin.
    6. Cover the burned area with a clean, dry, non-fluffy dressing or cloth.

    It's essential to keep the affected person calm and reassured while waiting for medical help to arrive. In addition, avoid applying any lotions, ointments, or home remedies to the burn, as this may increase the risk of infection.

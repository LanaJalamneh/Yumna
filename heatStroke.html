Heat Stroke

  • Basic information and Why it happened

    • Heat stroke is a medical emergency that occurs when the body's temperature regulation system fails due to prolonged exposure to high temperatures or physical exertion in hot and humid conditions.
    The body's core temperature rises to a dangerous level, causing damage to vital organs.
    Heat stroke can occur in people of all ages, but it's more common in older adults, children, and people with chronic medical conditions.

  • Prevention Advice

    • Preventing heat stroke involves taking appropriate measures to avoid exposure to high temperatures or physical exertion in hot and humid conditions.
    To prevent heat stroke, it's important to stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water and other fluids, especially when exercising or working in hot and humid conditions.
    Wear lightweight, light-colored, loose-fitting clothing and a wide-brimmed hat to protect yourself from the sun. Avoid strenuous activity during the hottest part of the day, and take frequent breaks in a cool, shady area.

  • Damage to the Body

    • Heat stroke can cause damage to vital organs such as the brain, heart, kidneys, and liver. It can also lead to seizures, coma, and even death if not treated promptly. The symptoms of heat stroke include a high body temperature (above 104Â°F), confusion, slurred speech, seizures, rapid heartbeat, and dry skin.

  • What to Do

    • If you suspect someone has heat stroke, it's important to take the following steps:

    1. Call for emergency medical help and Seek medical attention immediately.
    2. Move the person to a cool, shaded area.
    3. Remove excess clothing and wet the skin with cool water.
    4. Fan the person to promote sweating and cooling.
    5. Offer water or a sports drink if the person is conscious and able to drink.

    It's essential to keep the affected person calm and reassured while waiting for medical help to arrive. In addition, avoid giving the person anything to drink if they are unconscious, vomiting, or showing signs of confusion. Do not give aspirin or acetaminophen to reduce fever, as this can make the condition worse.

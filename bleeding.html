Bleeding

  • Basic information and Why it happened

    • Bleeding is the escape of blood from a damaged blood vessel, and it can occur for various reasons. Some common causes of bleeding include accidents, falls, cuts, lacerations, or surgical procedures.
    Bleeding can range from mild to severe and can be life-threatening if not treated promptly.

  • Prevention Advice

    • Preventing bleeding involves taking appropriate safety measures to avoid injuries. To prevent bleeding, it's important to use protective gear such as helmets, gloves, and other safety equipment when engaging in high-risk activities or when handling sharp tools and equipment.
    Additionally, practicing caution and awareness of potential hazards in your environment can reduce your risk of injury. Eating a balanced diet and engaging in regular exercise can help keep blood vessels healthy and reduce the risk of bleeding.

  • Damage to the Body

    • The damage caused by bleeding depends on the severity of the injury and the amount of blood lost. Excessive blood loss can lead to shock, a condition where the body's vital organs do not receive enough oxygen or nutrients. Shock can result in organ damage or failure, and in severe cases, even death. In addition, loss of blood can lead to anemia, a condition where the body lacks enough red blood cells to carry oxygen to tissues and organs.

  • What to Do

    • If you or someone else is bleeding, it's important to act quickly and take the following steps:

    1. Call for emergency medical help immediately.
    2. Apply pressure on the wound using a clean cloth or bandage.
    3. Elevate the affected body part above the heart if possible to reduce blood flow.
    4. If bleeding is severe, and it cannot be stopped by direct pressure, apply a tourniquet only as a last resort, and release it every 15-20 minutes for 1-2 minutes.
    5. Monitor the affected person's vital signs such as breathing and heart rate until medical help arrives.

    It's essential to keep the affected person calm and reassured while waiting for medical help to arrive. In addition, avoid removing any object that is embedded in the wound, as this may increase bleeding.

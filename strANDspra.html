Strains and Sprains

  • Basic information and Why it happened

    • Strains and sprains are common injuries that can occur when a muscle, tendon, or ligament is stretched or torn. These injuries can be caused by a variety of activities, including sports, exercise, and everyday activities such as lifting heavy objects or twisting the body. Strains and sprains can cause pain, swelling, and limited mobility.

  • Prevention Advice

    • Preventing strains and sprains involves taking appropriate precautions during physical activities. To prevent strains and sprains, it's important to warm up before exercise or physical activity, use proper techniques for lifting and carrying heavy objects, wear appropriate footwear, and take frequent breaks to rest and stretch during physical activity.

  • Damage to the Body

    • Strains and sprains can cause pain, swelling, bruising, and limited mobility. In severe cases, these injuries can require surgery and may lead to long-term complications.

  • What to Do

    • If you suspect someone has a strain or sprain, it's important to take the following steps:

    1. Rest the injured area and avoid any activity that causes pain.
    2. Apply ice to the injured area for 20 minutes at a time, several times a day.
    3. Use compression, such as an elastic bandage, to help reduce swelling.
    4. Elevate the injured area above the level of the heart to help reduce swelling.
    5. Take pain relievers such as acetaminophen or ibuprofen, as directed by a healthcare professional.

    It's important to seek medical attention if the injury is severe, or if there is significant pain or swelling. In some cases, imaging tests such as X-rays or MRI may be necessary to determine the extent of the injury.
    Treatment may include physical therapy, immobilization with a brace or cast, or in severe cases, surgery.

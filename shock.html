Shock

  • Basic information and Why it happened

    • Shock is a life-threatening medical emergency that occurs when the body's vital organs do not receive enough blood and oxygen. Shock can be caused by a variety of conditions, including severe bleeding, severe infections, severe allergic reactions, and heart failure.
    The symptoms of shock include rapid breathing, rapid heartbeat, pale or clammy skin, confusion, and loss of consciousness.

  • Prevention Advice

    • Preventing shock involves taking appropriate measures to avoid conditions that can cause it. To prevent shock, it's important to take appropriate safety measures to avoid injuries and infections.
    Practice good hygiene to avoid infections, and seek medical attention promptly for any injuries or infections. If you have a known medical condition that can cause shock, follow your healthcare provider's advice for managing the condition.

  • Damage to the Body

    • Shock can cause damage to vital organs such as the brain, heart, kidneys, and liver. It can also lead to coma and even death if not treated promptly.

  • What to Do

    • If you suspect someone has shock, it's important to take the following steps:

    1. Call for emergency medical help and Seek medical attention immediately.
    2. Have the person lie down with their legs elevated above the level of their heart.
    3. Cover the person with a blanket to keep them warm.
    4. Do not give the person anything to eat or drink.
    5. If the person vomits or is bleeding from the mouth, turn them onto their side to prevent choking.

    It's essential to keep the affected person calm and reassured while waiting for medical help to arrive. Do not move the person if you suspect they have a spinal injury, as this can cause further damage. If the person stops breathing or their heart stops beating, begin CPR immediately.

Unconscious and Breathing

  • Basic information and Why it happened

    • Unconsciousness can be caused by a variety of medical conditions, including head injury, stroke, seizures, drug overdose, and heart attack.
    If someone is unconscious and breathing, it's important to take immediate action to ensure their airway is open and they continue breathing.

  • Prevention Advice

    • Preventing unconsciousness involves taking appropriate precautions to avoid injuries or medical conditions that can lead to loss of consciousness.
    To prevent head injuries, wear appropriate protective gear during activities that carry a risk of head injury, such as sports or riding a bicycle or motorcycle. To prevent drug overdoses, avoid using drugs or alcohol, and always take medications as prescribed.

  • Damage to the Body

    • Unconsciousness can be a symptom of a serious medical condition that requires immediate attention.
    If someone is unconscious and not breathing, this can quickly lead to brain damage and even death if not treated promptly.

  • What to Do

    • If someone is unconscious and breathing, it's important to take the following steps:

    1. Check their airway to ensure it is open and not blocked. If necessary, gently tilt their head back to open the airway.
    2. Check their breathing by watching their chest for movement and listening for breath sounds. If they are not breathing, start CPR immediately.
    3. Call for emergency medical help, and provide any relevant information about the person's medical history or condition.
    4. If the person is lying on their back, gently roll them onto their side into the recovery position to prevent choking and ensure their airway remains open.
    5. Stay with the person and continue to monitor their breathing until medical help arrives.

    It's important to seek medical attention immediately if someone is unconscious, even if they are breathing. The underlying cause of unconsciousness can be serious and may require urgent medical treatment.
    If someone is not breathing, immediate CPR is essential to maintain blood flow and oxygen to the brain until medical help arrives.
